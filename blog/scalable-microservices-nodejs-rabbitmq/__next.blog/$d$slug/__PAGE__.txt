6:T116f,<p>As your application grows, a monolithic architecture becomes a bottleneck. Microservices allow you to scale parts of your app independently, but they require a robust way to communicate.</p>
<h2>Why RabbitMQ?</h2>
<p>RabbitMQ is a message broker that allows services to send messages to each other without needing to know if the other service is currently online. This is called <strong>asynchronous communication</strong>.</p>
<h2>Key Patterns:</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Work Queues</strong>: Distribute heavy tasks (like image processing) across multiple workers.</li>
<li><strong>Publish/Subscribe</strong>: Notify multiple services about an event (e.g., "User Created").</li>
<li><strong>Retry Logic</strong>: Automatically retry failed messages with exponential backoff.</li>
</ul>
