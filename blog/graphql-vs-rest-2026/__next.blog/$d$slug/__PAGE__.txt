6:T4bb,<p>The debate between GraphQL and REST has been raging for a decade. In 2026, the arrival of <strong>tRPC</strong> and <strong>Server Components</strong> has added new layers to this discussion.</p>
<h2>REST: The Universal Language</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Pros</strong>: Caching is built-in, predictable, works everywhere.</li>
<li><strong>Cons</strong>: Over-fetching (getting data you don't need) and multiple round-trips.</li>
</ul>
<h2>GraphQL: The Front-end's Dream</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Pros</strong>: Client gets exactly what it asks for, strongly typed, single endpoint.</li>
<li><strong>Cons</strong>: Complex to setup, caching is difficult, potential for N+1 query problems.</li>
</ul>
<h2>The New Contender: tRPC</h2>
<p>For TypeScript-only stacks, tRPC offers the benefits of GraphQL (type safety, no round trips) with zero of the complexity.</p>
<h2>When to use what?</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Public API</strong>: Use <strong>REST</strong>.</li>
<li><strong>Complex, multi-platform app</strong>: Use <strong>GraphQL</strong>.</li>
<li><strong>Internal Fullstack TS app</strong>: Use <strong>tRPC</strong>.</li>
</ul>
