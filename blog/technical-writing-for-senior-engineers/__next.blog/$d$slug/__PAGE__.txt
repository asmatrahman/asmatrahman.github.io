6:T44b,<p>As you move from Junior to Senior, your primary output shifts from <strong>code</strong> to <strong>communication</strong>. Technical writing is the skill that allows you to scale your impact across a whole organization.</p>
<h2>What should you write?</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>RFCs (Request for Comments)</strong>: Proposing new architectures or tools.</li>
<li><strong>Documentation</strong>: Ensuring your knowledge doesn't die when you leave the company.</li>
<li><strong>Blog Posts</strong>: Building your external authority and helping the community.</li>
<li><strong>Post-mortems</strong>: Analyzing what went wrong and how to fix it.</li>
</ul>
<h2>The Secret to Good Tech Writing:</h2>
<ol>
<li><strong>Know your audience</strong>: Are you writing for a CEO or a Dev?</li>
<li><strong>Be concise</strong>: Respect the reader's time.</li>
<li><strong>Use examples</strong>: abstract concepts need concrete snippets.</li>
<li><strong>Structure matters</strong>: Use headers, lists, and bold text effectively.</li>
</ol>
