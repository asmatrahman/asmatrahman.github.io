6:T4b4,<p>Large Language Models are brilliant, but they have a "knowledge cutoff." They don't know about your user's specific data or your company's internal documentation—unless you give it to them.</p>
<h2>The Problem with Context Windows</h2>
<p>While context windows are getting larger (up to 2M tokens), feeding every document into a prompt is expensive and slow.</p>
<h2>Enter Vector Databases</h2>
<p>Vector databases solve this by storing data as <strong>embeddings</strong>—mathematical representations of meaning.</p>
<h3>Key Benefits:</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Semantic Search</strong>: Find documents based on meaning, not just keywords.</li>
<li><strong>Scalability</strong>: Handle millions of documents with sub-second retrieval.</li>
<li><strong>Cost-Efficiency</strong>: Only send the most relevant snippets to the LLM.</li>
</ul>
<h3>Popular Choices in 2026:</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Pinecone</strong>: The serverless standard.</li>
<li><strong>Weaviate</strong>: Open-source and highly flexible.</li>
<li><strong>Chroma</strong>: Great for local development.</li>
</ul>
