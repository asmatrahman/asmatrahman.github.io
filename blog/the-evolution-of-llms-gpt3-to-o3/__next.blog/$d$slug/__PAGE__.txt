<h2>GPT-3: The Shot Heard 'Round the World</h2>
<p>When GPT-3 launched in 2020, it proved that scale was a viable path to intelligence. With 175 billion parameters, it could write poetry, code, and translate languages, albeit with frequent "hallucinations."</p>
<h2>GPT-4: The Gold Standard</h2>
<p>GPT-4 introduced multi-modality and a significant jump in reliability. It became the backbone of professional AI applications, from legal research to automated software engineering.</p>
<h2>The Reasoning Era: o1 and o3</h2>
<p>The release of OpenAI's <strong>o1</strong> and the recent <strong>o3</strong> marked a fundamental change. These models use <strong>Chain of Thought (CoT)</strong> reasoning to "think" before they speak.</p>
<h3>Why o3 Matters:</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>STEM Mastery</strong>: Near-perfect scores on competitive math and science tests.</li>
<li><strong>Deep Reasoning</strong>: Ability to break down multi-step coding tasks.</li>
<li><strong>Verification</strong>: Better self-correction during the generation process.</li>
</ul>
