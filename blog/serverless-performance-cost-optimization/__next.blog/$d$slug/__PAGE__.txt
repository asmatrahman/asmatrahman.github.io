6:T47e,<p>Serverless is amazing for agility, but if not managed correctly, it can lead to high "cold start" latency and surprise bills.</p>
<h2>1. Avoiding Cold Starts</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Keep Functions Small</strong>: Use fine-grained functions rather than a one-size-fits-all API.</li>
<li><strong>Pre-warming</strong>: Use tools to ping your functions and keep them "warm."</li>
<li><strong>Edge Functions</strong>: Deploy to the edge (Vercel/Cloudflare) for near-instant execution.</li>
</ul>
<h2>2. Reducing Costs</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Database Connection Pooling</strong>: Use a proxy like Prisma Accelerate to prevent exhausting database connections.</li>
<li><strong>Cache aggressively</strong>: Use Stale-While-Revalidate (SWR) headers to serve data from the CDN.</li>
<li><strong>Prune Dependencies</strong>: Don't import massive libraries into your serverless functions.</li>
</ul>
<h2>3. Monitoring</h2>
<p>Use <strong>Axiom</strong> or <strong>Datadog</strong> to track your execution times and identify the functions that are costing the most.</p>
